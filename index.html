TYTL provides institutional investors access to premium US single-family primary residence, in high-growth communities, with deed-recorded ownership, 1:1 tokenization, daily transparent valuation, and institutional-grade custody.

Primary single-family residential homes in top-quartile appreciating ZIP codes have historically been one of the most consistent appreciating asset classes in America. Until TYTL, there was no scalable, transparent, liquid instrument to access it at the individual property level. The TYTL token is that investment vehicle.

A new instrument

Mortgage-backed securities are debt-backed, pooled, and dependent on borrower repayment. REITs are pooled funds with dividend obligations. TYTL tokens are individual property level, appreciation-driven, and deed-recorded. No direct equivalent exists in the current market.

Institutional transparency

Every property has a unique Solana Program Derived Address. Portfolio revalued nightly by multiple independent AVMs. NAV published on-chain via Chainlink oracle. Property-level data publicly verifiable. Every claim is auditable.

The regulatory path

Currently available to institutions and accredited investors under Regulation D, with custody through Anchorage Digital Bank. TYTL will pursue Regulation A registration to broaden access and expand secondary market liquidity on Solana.

The TYTL Token

What is a TYTL Token?

A TYTL Token is a fully asset-backed digital security representing a pro-rata economic interest in a diversified portfolio of prime residential real estate equity and treasury-held cash assets. Each token is issued only after TYTL acquires a legally recorded fractional ownership interest in a qualifying home. Portfolio value and token pricing are calculated daily and recorded on-chain. TYTL Tokens are not debt instruments and do not provide dividends or guaranteed liquidity.

The title is on the chain

When people talk about tokenization, they often mean a contract that references an asset. With TYTL, the fractional ownership interest exists in the public property record. The blockchain is the transparency layer the deed is the foundation.

Daily NAV, on-chain

Portfolio revalued nightly using multiple independent AVMs. NAV published on-chain via Chainlink oracle infrastructure, alongside treasury balances held at Anchorage Digital Bank. Token value tracks the underlying real estate portfolio.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions

01What is TYTL?
TYTL is a blockchain-based platform that enables fractional ownership of prime US residential real estate in high-appreciation ZIP codes. Each TYTL token represents a direct pro-rata ownership interest in recorded property title across a diversified portfolio of premium single-family homes. Ownership is dual-recorded on-chain via Solana and legally on deed at the county level.
02How is TYTL different from a HELOC, HEI, or cash-out refinance?
TYTL is an equity purchase, not debt. With a HELOC, cash-out refinance, or HEI, you are borrowing against your home taking on repayment obligations, interest costs, and increasing financial risk. With TYTL, you are selling a fractional ownership interest. No monthly payments, no interest, no loan balance, no risk of foreclosure due to missed payments.
03Do homeowners give up control of their property?
No. Homeowners retain full control, occupancy, and decision-making rights. TYTL is a passive minority owner and does not interfere with how the property is used or maintained.
04Is a TYTL transaction taxable?
A TYTL transaction is generally treated as a partial sale of your home. In almost all cases, homeowners qualify for the IRS §121 capital gains exclusion up to $250,000 (individual) or $500,000 (married filing jointly). As a result, most homeowners owe little or no tax. Consult a qualified tax advisor for guidance specific to your situation.
05Can I buy back the equity I sold?
Yes. For up to three years, homeowners may repurchase TYTL's ownership stake at a discount to current market value. After three years, homeowners may buy back at the current Consensus Fair Market Value.
06How do TYTL tokens gain value over time?
Token value is derived from the Net Asset Value (NAV) of TYTL's underlying real estate portfolio and treasury holdings. NAV is updated daily using institutional-grade AVMs and published on-chain via Chainlink oracle infrastructure. TYTL acquires assets at a discount to CFMV and focuses on ZIP codes with strong historical appreciation (typically ≥6.5% long-term CAGR).
07How can I invest in TYTL?
TYTL is currently available to institutions and accredited investors only, tradable through custodial services at Anchorage Digital Bank. Broader access is planned through future regulatory pathways including Regulation A. Contact investors@tytl.ai for details.
